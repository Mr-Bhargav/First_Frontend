Leaders In Front

Leaders are individuals who inspire, motivate, and guide others towards a common goal or vision
using their skills, knowledge, and experience to bring about positive change in their organization or community

State Level Leaders

MLA/MP

"Member of Legislative Assembly." An MLA is a representative elected by the people of a particular constituency in a state to serve in the state legislature. The main responsibilities of an MLA include representing the interests and concerns of their constituency in the legislative assembly, participating in the lawmaking process, and overseeing the functioning of the state government. Each state in India has its own legislative assembly, and the number of MLAs in each assembly is determined by the population of the state.

District Level Leaders

ZPTCS/MYC/

ZPTC stands for "Zilla Parishad Territorial Constituency." It is a constituency for the election of members to the Zilla Parishad, which is the local government body at the district level. ZPTCs are created by dividing a district into territorial constituencies, and each ZPTC is represented by a member who is elected through a direct election by the voters of that constituency. The ZPTCs, along with the MPTCs (Mandal Parishad Territorial Constituencies), form the electoral college for the election of the Zilla Parishad Chairperson. The Zilla Parishad is responsible for the administration of various development programs and services at the district level, including agriculture, health, education, roads, and public works.

Mandal Level Leaders

MPP

MPP can stand for "Mandal Parishad President." Mandal Parishad is a local government body at the Mandal (block/tehsil) level in some states of India. The Mandal Parishad President (MPP) is the head of the Mandal Parishad and is elected by the members of the Mandal Parishad Territorial Constituencies (MPTCs). The MPP is responsible for the overall administration of the Mandal, including the implementation of various development programs and services, such as health, education, agriculture, roads, and public works. The MPP also represents the Mandal at higher levels of government and works closely with other local government bodies, such as the Zilla Parishad and Gram Panchayats, to ensure coordinated and effective delivery of services.
